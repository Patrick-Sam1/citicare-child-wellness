Tips to Feeling Better Mentally

Be sure to make time for a short, 5 minute break each hour to help you unwind and refocus, no matter what you're doing. Fitting at least a 30 minute break into your work day also gives you the opportunity to step away from stress and recharge.

Here are some additional strategies to support your mental well-being:

  • Practice deep breathing exercises for 5 minutes daily
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet
  • Connect with loved ones regularly
  • Engage in physical activity that you enjoy
  • Set realistic goals and celebrate small wins

Remember, mental health is just as important as physical health. Taking small steps each day can lead to significant improvements in how you feel.

How to Know if Therapy is Working

You've been having therapy for a while now. You like your therapist and things are beginning to feel a little lighter… But how do you know if your therapy is actually working?

Here are some signs that therapy is making a positive impact:

  • You're gaining new insights about yourself and your patterns
  • You feel more equipped to handle challenges
  • Your relationships are improving
  • You're experiencing fewer symptoms of distress
  • You're setting and achieving personal goals

Progress in therapy is often gradual. Trust the process and communicate openly with your therapist about how you're feeling.

January 5, 2026 | Mental Health

Can Depression Make You Sick?

There is a strong link between your brain and gastrointestinal system, therefore depression, anxiety, and stress can hinder the movement and contractions of your GI tract. Which can lead to various physical symptoms.

Depression can manifest in physical ways including:

  • Chronic fatigue and low energy
  • Changes in appetite and weight
  • Digestive issues and stomach pain
  • Headaches and muscle tension
  • Weakened immune system

If you're experiencing these symptoms alongside persistent sadness or loss of interest, it's important to seek professional help. Mental health is deeply connected to physical health, and addressing one can benefit the other.
